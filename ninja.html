Stealth

"Stealth" is an engaging 2D platformer that is a mix of action, movement precision, and strategic enemy encounters within a dynamic environment. In this project, I explored how to create a fluid and immersive gameplay experience that challenges players while maintaining fairness and responsiveness. I achieved this by implementing core mechanics such as enemy AI, dynamic platforming, player detection systems, animations, and environmental interactions.

One of the central mechanics I introduced is enemy logic, where enemies not only patrol the environment but also dynamically respond to player presence. They can detect the player using raycasts and alter their movement patterns accordingly.

The enemies in my game serve as dynamic obstacles that challenge the playerâ€™s movement, timing, and strategy. Each enemy has unique behaviors that force the player to adapt and interact with them in different ways.

  • The Camera Enemy is a stationary but dangerous foe that continuously scans its surroundings. It rotates within a limited angle range and, upon detecting the player, stops moving and begins firing projectiles with rapid precision. To avoid being spotted, the player must utilize movement strategies such as jumping, hiding, or using invisibility to slip past undetected.
  • The Red Ninjas are the most aggressive and relentless enemies in the game. They patrol platforms, detect the player from a distance, and chase with increased speed. Once they engage, they swiftly close the distance and strike with fast melee attacks. Their design includes edge detection to prevent them from falling off platforms, and they react dynamically to the player's position. When spotted, the player has to decide between fighting back, outmaneuvering them, or using invisibility to escape.

Technical Skills: Unity, C#
